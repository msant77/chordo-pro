Frequently Asked Questions

What file formats does Chordo Pro support?

Chordo Pro natively uses ICLF (Inline Chorded Lyrics Format), an open plain-text standard. You can also import ChordPro files, Ultimate Guitar format, and inline bracket notation. All imports are converted to clean ICLF.

Does Chordo Pro work offline?

Yes, 100%. Chordo Pro works entirely without an internet connection. All your data stays on your device — no account required, no cloud sync, no analytics.

How do I transfer my songs to another device?

Use the export feature to save your songs as .iclf files, then transfer them via AirDrop, email, USB, or any file-sharing method. ICLF files are plain text and work everywhere.

Which instruments are supported?

Guitar (6-string), 7-string guitar, ukulele, bass, banjo, mandolin, and custom tunings. Each instrument has voicings at multiple difficulty levels with an interactive fretboard.

Can I transpose a song to a different key?

Yes. Open any song in the viewer and use the transpose controls to shift all chords up or down by semitones. The original file is not modified — transposition is applied on the fly.

What platforms does Chordo Pro run on?

Chordo Pro is available for macOS, iOS, and iPadOS. Windows and Linux versions are planned.
